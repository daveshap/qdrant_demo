MARIETTE: But my dear M'sieu Giron, I'm having a tea party. Must I be bothered with all these papers now?
GIRON: I'm sorry, madame, but there are still certain matters which I think you should attend to yourself. And I, personally, would not care to refer them to--to--
MARIETTE: To M'sieu Laval?
GIRON: Madame Colet, I've enjoyed the confidence of your family for more than forty years. I was a school friend of your husband's ...
MARIETTE: I know!
GIRON: Madame, let me ask you: Who is M'sieu Laval, anyhow? Where does he come from? What is he?
MARIETTE: He is my secretary! I hope that answers all your questions, M'sieu Giron.
GIRON: You know what Paris is saying about the Countess Falconier and her chauffeur?
MARIETTE: Jacques!
