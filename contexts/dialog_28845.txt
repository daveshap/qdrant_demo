GASTON: That's hotel life. In one room a man loses his wallet and in another room a man loses-- his head.
LILY: Please. When I came here it was for a little adventure--a little game which you play tonight and forget tomorrow. But something has changed me--and it isn't the champagne. The whole thing is new to me. Very new. I've got a confession to make to you. Baron, you are a crook. You robbed the gentleman in two fifty-three, five, seven, and nine. May I have the salt?
GASTON: Please.
LILY: Thank you.
GASTON: The pepper, too?
LILY: No, thank you.
GASTON: You're very welcome. Countess, believe me, before you left this room I would have told you everything ... And let me say this with love in my heart-- Countess, you are a thief. The wallet of the gentleman in two fifty-three, five, seven, and nine is in your possession. I knew it very well when you took it out of my pocket. In fact, you tickled me.
