Celiac Plexus Block, NAME OF PROCEDURE:, Celiac plexus block.,ANESTHESIA:, Local/IV sedation.,COMPLICATIONS:, None.,PROCEDURE:, INT was placed. The patient was in the prone position back prepped and draped in sterile fashion. Then 1.5% of Lidocaine for skin wheal was made approximately 10 cm lateral to the L1-L2 vertebral junction. A 20 gauge, 15 cm needle was then placed in a cephalad medial 45o direction; the tip of the needle was just inside the L1 vertebral body. On lateral view, this was noted to be approximately 1.5-2.5 cm anterior to the vertebral body. At this time, 3 cc of Omnipaque dye was injected to the opposite side where the same sequence was performed. Following this, a mixture of 18 cc of 0.5% Marcaine was injected on each side Neosporin and band-aids was applied over the puncture sites. The patient was taken to the outpatient recovery where blood pressure was monitored and fluids given as needed.
