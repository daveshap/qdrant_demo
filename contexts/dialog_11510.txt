CLERK: The night clerk has already gone -- you are late.
SENF: Man -- I was at the clinic the whole night -- there are no words to describe what my wife suffered.
CLERK: And the child isn't coming?
SENF: No -- no -- not yet. Well, I mustn't let it interfere with my duty. Any news here?
CLERK: News? Yes -- killing in number one- sixty-four.
SENF: What? -- Who? -- Whom?
CLERK: The big manufacturer killed Baron von Gaigern.
SENF: Good heavens. What for?
CLERK: I don't know.
SENF: Man -- that's terrible. He was a nice fellow -- I am sorry about him.
CLERK: It seems that he was a thief and an imposter.
SENF: I don't believe it -- he was a real gentleman. I know people... I'm so tired I can hardly see out of my eyes. No sleep for two nights and so many duties and now this killing in the hotel -- that means a lot of work. But it's too bad about the Baron, you always felt better when he came along -- always friendly -- such an agreeable fellow.
